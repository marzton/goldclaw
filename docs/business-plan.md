# Goldshore Business Plan

## Executive Summary

Goldshore is a trust infrastructure and applied AI studio for people and small
organizations navigating unreliable digital markets: fake jobs, noisy recruiters,
opaque platforms, weak identity checks, and fragile business tooling.

The business combines practical services, managed infrastructure, and proof-based
credentialing. Goldshore can help customers build safer workflows while giving
early contributors a verifiable record of real work, real artifacts, and specific
skills demonstrated under review.

## Market Problem

Recruiting and freelance markets are increasingly noisy. Applicants face ghost
jobs, low-signal credential filters, AI-written resumes, unverifiable portfolios,
and recruiters who often cannot judge the work itself. Small businesses face the
same trust gap when hiring technical help, selecting tools, or validating whether
someone can actually deliver.

Goldshore's opening is to make work evidence visible: scoped assignments,
reviewed artifacts, signed attestations, and public or private proof trails that
show what a person can do.

## Core Offer

Goldshore offers three connected product lines:

1. Trust and security consulting for small businesses, creators, and operators.
2. Cloudflare-based infrastructure, agent tooling, and integration support.
3. Skill verification programs that turn real project work into evidence-backed
   certificates.

The third line is the new business-plan addition: a supervised work-exchange and
credential pathway.

## Volunteer-To-Certificate Program

Goldshore will pilot a "Certificate of Skill" program for contributors who want
verified experience more than a traditional job listing. Participants choose a
scoped Goldshore project, complete defined deliverables, receive feedback, and
earn a certificate that identifies the skill demonstrated and the evidence behind
it.

The certificate is not a job guarantee, degree, license, or blanket endorsement.
It is a signed record that the participant completed specific work to an observed
standard.

### What Contributors Receive

- A Goldshore Certificate of Skill naming the skill, level, date, reviewer, and
  evidence reviewed.
- A portfolio-ready artifact or case note, where confidentiality permits.
- A contribution ledger showing tasks completed, pull requests, documents,
  shipped assets, reviews, and operational outcomes.
- Practical feedback from Goldshore or partner reviewers.
- Optional references for contributors who meet reliability and quality standards.

### What Goldshore Receives

- Help with bounded, non-critical project work.
- A growing talent pool with observed proof of capability.
- Case studies and reusable workflows for trust, credentialing, and applied AI
  operations.
- A differentiated community model in a market flooded by fake job posts and
  shallow recruiting signals.

## Compliance Guardrails

Goldshore should not treat unpaid contributors as a substitute for ordinary
employees or contractors. If the work primarily benefits Goldshore as production
labor, the default path is paid work.

Unpaid participation must be structured as a compliant practicum, educational
experience, community contribution, or open-source contribution where the
participant is the primary beneficiary. Every project should have:

- A written scope with learning goals and expected artifacts.
- Clear disclosure that there is no promise of a job.
- No displacement of paid workers.
- Time caps and flexible scheduling.
- A reviewer assigned before work begins.
- Permission boundaries for private data, credentials, customer systems, and
  production infrastructure.
- A completion rubric that ties the certificate to observed evidence.

Before launching at scale, Goldshore should have counsel review the program under
FLSA, state labor law, internship rules, and any nonprofit/community-service
structure used for public-benefit work.

Compliance references:

- U.S. Department of Labor FLSA volunteer guidance:
  https://webapps.dol.gov/elaws/whd/flsa/docs/volunteers.asp
- U.S. Department of Labor Fact Sheet #71 on internship programs:
  https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/fact-sheets/71-flsa-internships

## Certificate Standard

Each certificate should include:

- Participant name.
- Skill track and assessed level.
- Scope of work completed.
- Evidence list: links, commit hashes, documents, tickets, demos, or private
  review notes.
- Reviewer name and role.
- Issue date and expiration or review window.
- Verification URL or QR code.
- Disclaimer: "This certificate verifies reviewed work evidence. It is not a
  license, degree, employment guarantee, or legal employment record."

## Operating Model

1. Publish a catalog of scoped projects.
2. Screen contributors for fit, availability, and consent.
3. Assign a reviewer and private work area.
4. Track work in GitHub, Drive, Linear, or a Goldshore ledger.
5. Review artifacts against the rubric.
6. Issue the certificate only when evidence meets the stated threshold.
7. Convert strong contributors into paid contractors, employees, or partner
   referrals where demand exists.

## Revenue Model

- Paid services remain the primary revenue engine.
- Certificates are free for approved contributors during the pilot.
- Sponsors, schools, workforce groups, or businesses may later pay for cohort
  review, infrastructure access, or verified project placement.
- Businesses can pay Goldshore for candidate proof review, work-sample design,
  and trust screening.

## Pilot Success Metrics

- Number of scoped projects completed.
- Percent of contributors earning certificates.
- Reviewer hours per certificate.
- Contributor satisfaction and portfolio reuse.
- Number of paid follow-on opportunities created.
- Number of customer workflows improved by contributor artifacts.
- No unresolved labor, privacy, or credentialing complaints.

## Launch Steps

1. Select three low-risk pilot projects.
2. Draft contributor agreement and reviewer rubric.
3. Build certificate template and verification URL pattern.
4. Recruit 5-10 pilot contributors.
5. Review legal posture before public marketing.
6. Publish first certificates only after evidence and consent are complete.
